Relationship to DieselMaxx — These Are Complementary, Not Competitive
DieselMaxx = passive electrostatic copper mesh device; improves atomization and combustion efficiency at the point of injection. Works on the physics of fuel delivery.
CFS Additive = chemical treatment; cleans injectors, improves cetane, scavenges water, stabilizes stored fuel. Works on the chemistry of the fuel itself.
CFS Tank Services = periodic physical maintenance; polishes fuel, removes water and microbes, ensures NFPA 110 compliance. Works on the condition of the fuel and tank.

All three address different failure modes. A VZW generator site could use all three simultaneously — clean fuel (CFS services), chemically optimized fuel (CFS additive), and better combustion (DieselMaxx) — without interference.

Track 1 — Tank Services Opportunity

NFPA 110 Section 8.3.8 requires annual fuel quality testing. NFPA 25 Section 8.3.4.1 requires annual fuel degradation testing per ASTM D975. STI SP001 tank inspections are a compliance requirement for aboveground storage tanks. VZW generators average 21.3 hrs/yr — fuel sits in tanks for 11+ months between significant runs, creating ideal conditions for microbial growth, water accumulation, and sludge. Most sites are likely never serviced for fuel quality unless a generator fails to start. 39,521 diesel sites = direct addressable market. Annual contract model preferred.

Track 2 — Additive Opportunity

At 1:3000 treat ratio, Critical Power + Fleet treats 3,000 gallons per unit of additive. At 74.6 gal/yr average per site, one treatment per refueling covers the entire year at most sites. Claims: 5% typical fuel economy improvement, up to 70% emissions reduction, cetane +8, injector protection. Key question: does the additive need to be re-dosed each refueling cycle or does one dose stabilize the full tank? For fleet vehicles, GasMaxx (gasoline) and DieselMaxx applications overlap — need to understand product differentiation vs. DieselMaxx across the fleet vehicle mix.

Three-Stage Filtration Process

Stage 1 — Particulates
Primary bag filters (1–5 micron absolute). 1–7 filter housings with pressure gauges. Removes solids and microbial contamination per circulation. Stainless steel housing (no rust contamination).
Stage 2 — Water Coalescing
Aviation fuel grade micro-glass coalescer. Pleated cellulose inner filter with polyester glass fibers. Inside-to-outside flow coalesces tiny water droplets into larger drops for separation. Same process used for military jet fuel.
Stage 3 — Water Separator
Stainless steel/Teflon hydrophobic water separator cartridge. Physical barrier prevents any water from passing through with fuel. Result exceeds ISO 18/16/13 cleanliness standard (engine manufacturer recommended spec).

8. Recommended Pilot Structure

  1. Track 1 Pilot — Tank Services (Priority 1) — Select one VZW market with a high concentration of diesel generator sites (Carolina/Tennessee or Central Gulf Coast — both ~3,200–3,600 diesel sites). Commission CFS to perform NFPA 110 annual fuel testing + STI SP001 inspection on 50–100 sites. Document baseline fuel quality findings. This is immediately actionable — NFPA 110 compliance is a real requirement, and CFS either performs or doesn't. No methodology dispute possible.
  2. Track 2 Pilot — Additive (Priority 2, concurrent or sequential) — Select 20–30 generator sites with clean SiteBoss fuel consumption baselines. Divide into treated (Critical Power + Fleet dosed at each refueling) and control groups. Run 12 months minimum (to capture full maintenance run cycles). Measure gallons consumed per run-hour. Require CFS/Hydrotex to provide full independent test data before expanding beyond pilot.
  3. DieselMaxx Coordination — If DieselMaxx pilot is running concurrently on any of the same sites, discuss with both vendors whether co-deployment is appropriate or if isolation is needed to attribute savings. Goal is compatible deployment — not competitive testing.
  4. Bundled Model Evaluation — If both Track 1 and Track 2 show positive results, evaluate a bundled annual service contract: CFS visits each diesel site once per year, performs NFPA 110 testing, polishes fuel, doses tank with Critical Power + Fleet (or Cold Climate by region), and delivers compliance documentation. One vendor, one visit, one contract covering regulatory requirement + fuel optimization.
Why Tank Services First: The NFPA 110 compliance angle is a pull, not a push — VZW has a regulatory obligation to test fuel annually, and many sites are likely non-compliant. CFS performing inspections is immediately defensible to management and procurement without requiring a performance study. If they execute well on the inspection program, the additive add-on becomes a natural extension.

9. Flags to Watch

Do not proceed to scale without resolving:
  • Geographic coverage gaps — CFS offices are in Charlotte, Charleston, Tampa, Dallas, Las Vegas. Large VZW markets (Mountain Plains, Great Plains, Pacific Northwest, New England, New York) may have limited CFS coverage — confirm field capacity before committing to a national program
  • Subbase belly tank experience — Most VZW gen tanks are integral subbase tanks, not standalone ASTs. STI SP001 is primarily for ASTs — confirm certification and procedure applies to subbase configurations
  • Additive independent data — "100+ engine study" and Peugeot DW10B test are not SAE J1321 equivalent; need full methodology before citing 5–10% savings in any VZW business case
  • OEM warranty confirmation — Written confirmation from Generac, CAT, Kohler, and Cummins that additive use does not void warranty is required before any fleet deployment
  • DieselMaxx compatibility — Must confirm no interference between CFS chemical additive and DieselMaxx electrostatic mechanism before co-deploying on the same sites
  • NFPA 110 documentation format — CFS must produce compliance-grade documentation (FSR reports) that satisfy VZW's facilities management and any third-party auditors; confirm format meets VZW requirements
