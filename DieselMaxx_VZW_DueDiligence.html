1. What We Are Evaluating

DieselMaxx / GasMaxx is an inline fuel conditioning device installed between the fuel tank and injectors. The vendor claims it improves combustion efficiency, reducing fuel consumption by 8–25% (independent technical report range: 5–15%) and cutting carbon emissions by 85–94% across diesel generators, diesel trucks, and gasoline fleet vehicles.

✓ Mechanism Confirmed — NDA Technical Documents (received 2026-05-26)

Two technical documents received under NDA describe and independently support the operating mechanism:

  • Active element: Steel tube containing copper mesh — no magnets, no chemicals, no electronics. Completely passive.
  • Mechanism: Turbulent flow through copper mesh generates electrostatic charge on the fuel (flow electrification / streaming current — a documented phenomenon in fuel handling safety literature). The charged fuel breaks Van der Waals / hydrogen bonds between fuel molecules → smaller droplet diameter → better air/fuel mixing → more complete combustion → fuel savings and reduced emissions.
  • Charge persistence: Fuel retains electrostatic charge from the device through the delivery system into the engine. Effect is pre-combustion and independent of engine brand (Generac, CAT, Kohler, Cummins).
  • Independent support: 4 SAE technical papers cited (SAE 2000-01-2041, SAE 2002-01-3053, SAE 972987, SAE 2005-32-0090) confirming electrostatic charge improves diesel atomization.

Document authors: Richard Carlson, M.S. Environmental Engineering (UCLA), 25 yrs at independent vehicle/engine testing labs, SAE member; and Erin McBride, Siemens Senior Planning Engineer (independent of vendor).
Note: Carlson's independent report cites a more conservative 5–15% savings range vs. the vendor deck's 8–25% — use 5–15% as the basis for pilot success criteria.

⚠ Methodology Flags — Review Before Citing This Data:
  • Load was not equal between tests. Test 1 produced 10 kWh; Test 2 produced 11 kWh — 10% more electrical output. A valid controlled comparison requires identical load. When normalized for output, specific fuel consumption improved ~22% with ECO, but the unequal loads make either figure unreliable until explained.
  • kWh readings appear inconsistent with 50% load. At 50% of 125 kVA (~50 kW real power), expected output over 1 hour ≈ 50 kWh. Actual measured: 10–11 kWh — roughly 80% below expected. Either the load was far below 50% of rated, or the kWh meter is measuring a single phase of a 3-phase output. Must be clarified before citing the savings figure.
  • Not an independent test lab. Sinewave Generators Private Limited is an Authorized Cummins Dealer — operationally credible but not a neutral third-party testing facility.
  • Fuel measurement resolution. Fuel measured in whole litres only. ±1 litre uncertainty = ±7.1% on a 14-litre measurement — the 2-litre claimed saving is within 2× the measurement error band.
  • Single run, no replication. One test run each direction; no statistical confidence interval possible.
  • Warm-up asymmetry. Test 1 had a 10-minute warm-up (cold-start fuel penalty absorbed before measurement); Test 2 ran on an already-warm engine — a slight fuel advantage built into the ECO baseline.
  • Emissions testing pending. The report explicitly notes carbon emissions testing is still to be done — no emissions data from this test.

Bottom line: Direction is correct and the 14.28% magnitude aligns with the conservative 5–15% technical range from the Carlson independent report. Real equipment, real serial number, and a Cummins-authorized tester gives this more weight than a vendor case-study slide. However, the unequal kWh output and unexplained kWh magnitude are legitimate methodological issues. Use as supporting evidence alongside the pilot — not primary proof.

5. Technology & Science

✓ Q1, Q2, Q3 below — answered by NDA technical documents; Q2 SAE citations independently verified on SAE Mobilus (2026-05-26). Questions remain in the list for formal vendor confirmation; outstanding questions begin at Q4.
  1. [ANSWERED] What is the underlying mechanism of action — is this a catalytic, magnetic, ionization, or thermal-based technology, and what physical or chemical change does it produce in the fuel before combustion? BOTH TECH
    Answer (per NDA docs): Electrostatic charging via turbulent flow through copper mesh. Flow electrification generates streaming current; charged fuel breaks molecular hydrogen bonds, producing smaller droplets and improved atomization. Mechanism rules out magnetic, catalytic, chemical, or external electrical explanations.

    Answer (per NDA docs + independent verification via SAE Mobilus, 2026-05-26): All 4 cited SAE papers confirmed real, titles accurate, subjects on-point for electrostatic fuel atomization.

11. Recommended Pilot Structure

Before any network-wide or fleet-wide commitment, Verizon would require a controlled pilot with documented methodology. Suggested structure:

  1. Generator Pilot (Priority 1) — Select 10–15 sites with clean fuel consumption baselines (subbase tank with calibrated level sensors already reporting to SiteBoss). Install DieselMaxx on half; leave half as controls. Run 90 days. Compare gallons consumed per run-hour, matched for load and ambient temperature.
  2. Fleet Pilot (Priority 2) — Matched-pair test: same vehicle class, same route geography, same driver population, half with device / half without. 60–90 days. Measure gal/100 miles. Eliminate seasonal and payload variables through randomization.
  3. Emissions Verification — If generator pilot shows fuel savings, engage an independent emissions lab to verify carbon reduction claims with calibrated instruments. This supports any VZW sustainability reporting if claims hold.
  4. Fleet Procurement Review — Regardless of pilot results, any device installed in a VZW fleet vehicle must clear Fleet Operations and Fleet Procurement review for warranty and liability implications.
  5. Scale Decision — If pilot validates savings at even the low end (8%), the generator ROI alone justifies rapid scale-up. Full network deployment decision by Q1 2027.
Why generators first: Generator fuel consumption is precisely measurable — gallons in, run-hours out, load known. It eliminates the driver behavior, route, payload, and traffic variables that make fleet fuel economy claims difficult to control. A clean generator pilot result is the fastest path to a credible go/no-go decision.
