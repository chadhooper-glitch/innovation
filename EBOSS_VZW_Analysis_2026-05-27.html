1. What Is EBOSS

EBOSS (Energy Boss, by ANA — Alliance North America, Inc., Henderson NV) is a trailer-mounted hybrid generator system. It pairs a diesel genset with an industrial LTO battery pack and inverter. The battery handles continuous load; the generator only starts to recharge batteries or cover load spikes. The result: dramatically reduced engine runtime, fuel consumption, and emissions vs. a conventional running generator.

The Verizon connection: a single EBOSS 70-45 unit has been running at Maloney Ridge Communications Site in PNW (WA) since late November 2025, shared among Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile, BPA, and BNSF under a Multi-Carrier Real Estate Use (MREU) agreement coordinated by Lumina Solutions. Jason Epling (AT&T Mobility, Power & Infrastructure Planning) forwarded the performance data directly.

VZW Initial Deployment Strategy — Deployable Asset Only
EBOSS will not replace stationary generators in this phase. The initial VZW use case is a trailer-mounted deployable fleet used in two scenarios:
  1. Emergency / storm response — dispatch to sites where the fixed generator has failed, fuel can't be delivered, or the site needs to be restored faster than the permanent power solution allows. EBOSS gets the site on air; the stationary gen stays in place.
  2. Sites awaiting utility — new builds that are constructed and ready but waiting on utility connection (30–180+ days is common). EBOSS replaces a rental genset for that window, then redeploys to the next waiting site after cutover.
In both cases the trailer moves. It is not a permanent installation. This keeps capital exposure low and maximizes utilization across the fleet.
⚠ Important Distinction: Maloney Ridge is a prime power site — EBOSS is the primary power source 24/7. Verizon's generator fleet is predominantly standby — generators that sit idle and run during grid outages or monthly maintenance tests. The economics are materially different. EBOSS makes most sense for VZW at sites with elevated runtime — or as a deployable asset as described above.

7. Due Diligence Questions ✓ NDA Executed 2026-06-08

  1. What is the all-in delivered cost for an EBOSS 70-45 unit (trailer + generator + battery module)? Is there a lease/PPA model available, or is it purchase only? What does enterprise volume pricing look like at 10, 50, and 500 units?
  2. For a VZW-owned site (not shared), what is the typical installation scope — site prep, utility connections, fuel line, transfer switch integration — and who performs the installation?
  3. Does the EBOSS replace or work alongside an existing standby generator? If an existing CAT or Generac is on-site, can the EBOSS serve as the primary with the existing gen as backup, or does it require removal of existing equipment?
  4. What is the EBOSS integration with SiteBoss / SNMP monitoring? Does ANA telematics expose OIDs or API endpoints that feed into existing VZW network management systems?
  5. What are the fuel management logistics — does the EBOSS 80.5-gal integral tank feed directly from the same fuel supply as an existing on-site generator, or does it require a dedicated supply?
  6. What are the CARB / EPA permitting implications of deploying an EBOSS at a CA site? The Airman SDG45 is Tier 4 / CARB-rated, but are there AQMD permit requirements for a trailer-mounted prime-power diesel in Southern California or Bay Area air districts?
  7. What is the warranty and service response SLA for a remote site in Alaska or Hawaii? ANA has offices in NV and CA — what is the field service model outside those geographies?
  8. Is parallel operation supported (two EBOSS units at a single site for N+1 reliability)? The spec sheet lists "Parallel Capability: Available" — what is the control architecture?
  9. ESG reporting — what format and cadence does ANA provide? Does it map to Verizon's existing emissions reporting framework (GHG Protocol, CDP)?
  10. What is ANA's production capacity and typical lead time from PO to delivery? If VZW identifies 50–100 priority remote sites, can ANA fulfill within 12 months?
  11. For the sites-awaiting-utility use case specifically — is there a short-term rental or redeployment model, or is it purchase only? VZW's intent is to use EBOSS as a deployable asset that moves from site to site as each gets utility cutover. Does ANA support that model contractually and logistically, and what does redeployment cost/logistics look like?
  12. For emergency fleet deployment — does ANA operate a managed rental fleet that VZW could call on during a major storm event, or does VZW need to own and pre-position units? What is typical dispatch time and mobilization cost for an emergency deployment in the Gulf Coast, Carolinas, or Florida?
  13. VZW cell sites run -48V DC power plants (rectifiers, batteries). EBOSS outputs 120/240V AC. Exactly how does EBOSS feed a VZW site — through the commercial AC input to the existing rectifiers, or is there a direct DC integration path? What is the transfer switch / ATS architecture for a site that also has a permanent standby generator that stays in place?
  14. The case studies reference multiple model designations — EBOSS 25-25, EBOSS 70-45, EBOSS 70 kVA, EBOSS 220-125, EBOSS220. What is the current model lineup, and what are the specs and pricing for each? Which model is recommended for a typical VZW macro site drawing 5–10 kW?
  15. The vendor states performance data comes from three years of field telematics. Under NDA, can ANA provide the full dataset — fleet size, site types, runtime distributions, and performance by climate zone — so VZW can independently validate savings projections for our specific site mix?
  16. What does real-world LTO battery capacity retention look like after 2–3 years of field operation? The spec sheet shows 90K cycle rating, but what is actual measured capacity at year 1, 2, and 3 in deployed units? Is there field data from the oldest units in service?
