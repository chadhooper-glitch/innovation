Verizon operates approximately 38,000 cell site shelters cooled by wall-pack HVAC units (Bard, Marvair, Airsys) sized to NSTD1000's legacy full-equipment load. Modern sites with external RRHs carry only 2–4 kW of actual heat load — yet the installed 5-ton Bard HR58BPA05EP delivers 3.8-ton actual sensible capacity (46,330 BTU/hr, 5.1 kW input) at NSTD1000's 11 EER rating. The result is a 2–3× oversized cooling plant running in short-cycle dead band (on at 81°F, off at 75°F), consuming compressor energy to cool largely empty shelter space.

This initiative installs an Eaton SRCOOL3KTP 33U self-cooling cabinet inside the shelter and migrates the remaining equipment into it. The Eaton cabinet's DC-inverter variable-speed compressor provides close-coupled precision cooling matched to the actual equipment heat load (<3.5 kW). The wall-pack WPUs are then placed in standby at a raised setpoint (~90°F) — available for humidity control and emergency backup per NSTD1000 N+1 requirements — with compressor runtime reduced to near-zero under normal conditions. A SiteBoss Lua script polls the Eaton cabinet every 5 minutes via SNMP (WEBCARDLX LX Platform) and controls setpoints via the PADM20 HTTP REST API. WattNode AC metering and CT current sensors on the SiteBoss provide before/after kWh measurement.

✓ Close-coupled precision cooling — Eaton variable-speed compressor modulates to actual heat load; avg draw 0.7–1.0 kW vs. 5.1 kW per WPU at full load.
✓ WPU duty cycle eliminated — wall-pack compressor runtime drops from ~25–50% annual duty to <5% emergency/humidity backup only.
✓ Measured savings, not modeled — WattNode on ILC + CT sensors on WPU circuits provide independent before/after kWh data via SiteBoss.

⚠ Setpoint Gap: Factory default cooling setpoints on Eaton/Tripp-Lite units are typically 65–68°F. Verizon's NSTD thermal standard permits 77°F return air in most cabinet configurations. Each 1°F increase in cooling setpoint saves approximately 2–3% of HVAC energy (ASHRAE guideline). A 9°F setpoint raise represents a potential 18–27% cooling energy reduction per site.

3. Solution — SiteBoss Lua Script

The script runs inside the SiteBoss 550A Lua slot and communicates with the WEBCARDLX card over the cabinet's internal Ethernet. No VPN or WAN access to the HVAC unit is needed — the SiteBoss acts as the local proxy.

SNMP Layer — Monitoring SNMP READ ONLY

SNMPv2c GET against enterprise OID 1.3.6.1.4.1.850.1.1.3.5 (Tripp-Lite cooling). Full BER encoder/decoder in pure Lua using LuaSocket UDP — no external SNMP library required. 20 OIDs polled in a single GET request every 5 minutes:

NSTD1000 Compliance Note

⚠ Deviation Required: NSTD1000 (rev. 04-22-26) requires a 79°F shelter setpoint. Raising WPU setpoint to ~90°F for this trial requires a formal deviation request to HQ EOS. Basis for deviation: NSTD1000 §2.18 specifies the critical temperature is "at the entering air conditions to the electronics" — all electronics are close-coupled cooled by the Eaton cabinet regardless of shelter ambient. N+1 redundancy is maintained: both Bard WPUs remain operational in standby. Trial duration: 60 days. Deviation scope: DT Morris (MDG_LI 5000092014) only.
