SPIN Energy Systems — VZW Feasibility Analysis

Chad Hooper  |  National CP&G Planning  |  chad.hooper@verizonwireless.com  |  2026-05-18

1. What Is SPIN

SPIN Energy Systems uses a Halbach array magnetic levitation flywheel to store kinetic energy. The rotor is magnetically suspended — near-frictionless — and spins at high speed to store energy. During a grid outage, the rotor spins down and the integrated motor/generator converts kinetic energy back to AC output.

The system is regenerative, similar to Tesla regenerative braking: during normal grid operation, load fluctuations and transitions allow the flywheel to recapture energy, reducing net grid energy draw. It is not a chemical battery — there is no degradation, no replacement cycle, and no hazardous materials.

  • Output: AC — fits upstream of panel schedule, feeds rectifiers; does not replace the −48VDC bus directly
  • Capacity: 10–15 kWh per cube (usable; confirm exact value with SPIN)
  • Scalable: Cubes are stackable for additional kWh and/or kW output
  • Maintenance: Near-zero — no chemistry, no watering, no load testing, no replacement cycle

2. Strategic Goal

Eliminate VRLA and NiCd battery strings from the VZW macro network entirely.

Battery-related costs per macro site over 20 years include:
  • VRLA string replacement every 5–7 years ($8,000–$15,000/string installed)
  • Annual capacity testing — load bank, truck roll
  • NiCd: electrolyte watering, cadmium hazmat disposal
  • Failed capacity test = outage risk + NORS filing (47 CFR Part 4)
  • End-of-life disposal/recycling costs
A SPIN flywheel that never needs replacement eliminates all of the above across tens of thousands of sites.

6. Open Questions — Get Answers from SPIN Under NDA

  1. Confirmed usable kWh per cube — is 10–15 kWh the full range, or does it vary by discharge rate?
  2. Maximum continuous kW output per cube — must meet full site load (8–12 kW)
  3. Parallel output — can cubes be combined for higher kW delivery, not just more kWh?
  4. Self-discharge rate — how many hours does a fully charged cube coast with no grid power? (Critical for no-gen sites)
  5. Operating ambient temperature range — must cover NEBS GR-63 envelope
  6. AC input spec — voltage, phase, current draw during charge/maintain
  7. AC output spec — voltage, phase, waveform quality (rectifiers are sensitive to THD)
  8. Recharge time — after partial or full discharge, how long to return to full capacity?
  9. Physical dimensions and weight per cube — shelter floor loading is a real constraint
  10. Seismic rating — VZW sites in Zone 4 (California) require GR-63 seismic compliance
  11. MTBF / warranty / service model — what does "no maintenance" actually mean contractually?

7. Path to Deployment

  1. NDA — share site configurations; get confirmed SPIN specs in return
  2. Lab / bench test — validate AC output quality, self-discharge, temp performance
  3. Pilot (3–5 sites) — varied climate zones, with and without generator
  4. NSTD47 deviation or update — NPRC776 deviation request or Central Engineering update to permit flywheel as qualifying backup power
  5. VZW procurement / contract — sourcing approval, pricing, nationwide rollout
